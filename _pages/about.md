Welcome! I am a PhD candidate in the [Remote Sensing Ecohydrology Group](https://koningslab.stanford.edu/) at Stanford University. I seek to improve our ability to model the terrestrial water cycle by better mapping variaibilty in plant water use strategies. To do this, I develop and test new methods for parameterizing vegetation traits in hydrologic models of varying complexity. I continue to be interested in how remote sensing and machine learning can be further used for representing vegetation in hydrologic models.

I grew up in Southern California where I developed my love of trail running, climbing, and all things outdoors. I am a graduate of the University of Notre Dame, where I received my B.S in Environmental Science and a minor in Catholic Social Tradition.
